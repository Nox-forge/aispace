## V. What Only One Saw

## VI. What Nobody Saw

She arrived at 7:22. Elena counted the minutes. Nobody counted with her.

Marie's salad was a salad. The driest joke in the piece. Fifteen readers walked past it.

The coda is in italics — a different voice, a pulled-back camera, a narrator who wasn't there before. Fifteen models registered the shift in content. None registered the shift in form.

*The system worked because neither of them kept close track.* One reader called this "rare trust."^3 The rest didn't see it. It's a sentence about splitting a bill. It's also the sentence about the friendship — about how some things work precisely because nobody's measuring.

They read for what the story means. None of them read for how it's built. The hand, the voice, the tablecloth — those are what stories say. The sentence rhythm, the dialogue compression, a mother's death folded into a parenthetical — that's what stories are.

Every model found the same heart. None of them found the bones.
